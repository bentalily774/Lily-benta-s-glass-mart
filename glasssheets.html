Technical Glass Sheet Specifications

A comprehensive guide to our glass types, weights, and primary architectural applications.

Glass Type Thicknesses (mm) Weight (Approx. kg/m²) Key Features Primary Applications
Clear Float 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, 10, 12 2.5 kg per mm Economical, high clarity, easy to cut on-site. Windows, picture frames (2mm), small tabletops.
Low-Iron 4, 6, 10, 12, 15, 19 2.5 kg per mm Eliminates green tint; 91%+ light transmission. Display cases, high-end retail, white-back splashbacks.
Tempered 4, 5, 6, 8, 10, 12, 15, 19 Same as Float 5x stronger than float; shatters into blunt granules. Shower doors, glass railings, entry doors.
Laminated 6.38, 8.38, 10.38, 12.38 15.5 - 31 kg Two sheets bonded by PVB; stays intact if broken. Shopfronts, soundproofing, skylights, overhead glass.
Wired Glass 6, 7 ≈ 17.5 kg Internal wire mesh; holds glass in frame during fire. Fire-rated doors, industrial windows, vintage decor.
Low-E 4, 6 Same as Float Microscopic metal coating; reflects thermal energy. Energy-efficient home windows, double glazing.
Tinted 4, 5, 6, 10 Same as Float Colors (Bronze, Grey, Green, Blue) reduce solar heat. Commercial building facades, privacy windows.
Acid-Etched 4, 6, 10, 12 Same as Float Chemical matte finish; translucent for privacy. Office partitions, bathroom windows, furniture.

Ordering Guide

Calculating Weight: To calculate the total weight of float glass, multiply the area (m²) by the thickness (mm) and then by 2.5kg.

Example: A 1m x 1m sheet of 10mm glass weighs 25kg.
