Bottles & Containers Catalog

Providing specialized glass packaging for food, beauty, and industrial sectors.

1. Beverages & Spirits

Container Type Key Features Best Used For
Boston Round Rounded shoulders, thick glass, narrow neck. Syrups, juices, wellness shots, cold brew.
Swing-Top (Fido) Built-in ceramic/plastic stopper with wire bale. Kombucha, beer, carbonated drinks, infused oils.
Wine Bottles Tall; Bordeaux (high shoulder) or Burgundy (sloped). Wine, spirits, dessert vinegars, decorative vases.
Flasks Flat, rectangular shape with curved edges. Spirits, high-end extracts, craft coffee concentrates.

2. Food & Preservation

Container Type Key Features Best Used For
Mason Jars Threaded neck, heat-resistant, traditional look. Canning, pickling, overnight oats, DIY gifts.
Hexagon Jars Six-sided geometric walls, premium aesthetic. Gourmet honey, jams, spices, artisanal chutneys.
Straight-Sided Jars Wide mouth, no "neck" or shoulders. Salsas, pâtés, candle making, cosmetic balms.
Orcio Jars Distinctive "curvy" urn shape. High-end olives, pickled vegetables, specialty oils.

3. Beauty & Apothecary

Container Type Key Features Best Used For
Dropper Bottles Small capacity, usually paired with glass pipettes. Essential oils, facial serums, eye drops.
Roll-on Bottles Slim, portable vials with ball applicators. Perfumes, aromatherapy blends, lip gloss.
Pump Bottles Heavy-duty base with wide neck for pumps. Lotions, liquid soaps, foundation, hair serums.
Amber/Cobalt Vials Dark colored glass for 99% UV protection. Vitamins, light-sensitive chemicals, medicine.

4. Storage & Decorative

Container Type Key Features Best Used For
Apothecary Jars Large, ornate jars with matching glass stoppers. Bath salts, candy displays, luxury home decor.
Hermetic Jars Heavy glass with rubber gasket and metal clamp. Bulk pantry storage (flour, pasta, coffee beans).
Carboys/Demijohns Massive bulbous jugs (often 1–5 gallons). Fermentation, large scale terrariums, floor vases.
