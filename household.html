Household Glass Collection

Premium glass solutions for every corner of your home, from the kitchen to the living room furniture.

1. Kitchen & Dining

Product Type Key Features Primary Use Case
Borosilicate Bakeware Thermal shock resistant; can go from freezer to oven. Casseroles, roasting, and food reheating.
Glass Drinkware Includes tumblers, highballs, and stemware (wine/flutes). Daily hydration, formal dining, and hosting.
Mixing Bowls Deep-welled, tempered glass for impact resistance. Food prep, baking, and serving salads.
Serving Pitchers Features pouring spouts and ergonomic handles. Serving iced tea, water, or sangria at the table.

2. Living Room & Decor

Product Type Key Features Primary Use Case
Glass Vases Available in "Bud," "Cylinder," or "Trumpet" shapes. Floral arrangements and centerpiece displays.
Hurricane Lamps Tall, open-ended glass cylinders to protect flames. Patio lighting and large pillar candle displays.
Glass Cloches Bell-shaped protective domes with a base. Displaying desserts, plants, or collectibles.
Decorative Trays Flat, polished glass with beveled or metallic edges. Organizing perfumes, jewelry, or coffee table books.

3. Bathroom & Vanity

Product Type Key Features Primary Use Case
Pump Dispensers Heavy-bottomed glass with corrosion-resistant pumps. Hand soap, lotion, or luxury dish soap.
Apothecary Canisters Small, lidded jars designed for easy access. Organizing cotton balls, swabs, and bath salts.
Wall Mirrors Reflective sheets with polished or framed edges. Vanity use, hallway decor, and brightening rooms.

4. Furniture & Storage

Product Type Key Features Primary Use Case
Tempered Tabletops Thick (usually 6mm - 12mm), impact-resistant glass. Protecting wooden desks or as standalone tops.
Floating Shelves High-load tempered glass with polished "pencil" edges. Minimalist storage in kitchens or bathrooms.
Cabinet Inserts Patterned or "Seeded" (bubbled) glass sheets. Replacing wooden cabinet panels for a modern look.
